# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Internal enterprise teams at organizations that have deployed AURA as their closed AI assistant platform. Users are employees and team members who work collaboratively around AI-generated output. They return to the same workflows daily: starting a chat, generating a document (report, decision brief, timeline, checklist), reviewing AI answers, and sharing results within the team.

They are not casual or first-time AI users. They are professionals who have context about their domain and expect the tool to keep up, not explain itself. The primary task on any given screen is either composing a message or reviewing and editing an AI-generated artifact.

## Product Purpose

AURA is an enterprise-grade AI assistant built for internal organizational use. It replaces scattered AI tool subscriptions and fragmented document workflows with one cohesive platform. Users can chat with AI agents, generate structured documents (reports, timelines, decision briefs, quizzes), process uploaded files, and collaborate within a shared workspace.

Success means teams trust AURA with their real work and find it indispensable within their daily workflows. The measure is adoption depth, not breadth: does the power user reach for AURA first?

## Brand Personality

Intelligent and elevated. AURA should feel like working with a brilliant collaborator, not operating a piece of software. The brand carries quiet confidence: no need to announce its capabilities, just demonstrate them. Premium but never cold. Precise without being clinical. The voice is expert-to-expert.

Three-word summary: **sharp, composed, trustworthy**.

Emotional goals: users should feel capable, focused, and in control. The interface amplifies their intelligence rather than competing with it.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS dashboards**: blue-primary cards, metric-widget grids, enterprise boilerplate. AURA is not a reporting tool and should not look like one.
- **Consumer chatbots**: bubbly rounded UI, gamified color gradients, patronizing onboarding copy.
- **ChatGPT clones**: the generic white-card, left-sidebar, monochrome layout that every AI tool defaults to.

## Design Principles

1. **The interface recedes into the work.** The AI output and the user's own content are the primary visual objects. Decoration that competes with the content is decoration that undermines the product.

2. **Craft earns trust.** Enterprise users are skeptical. Every detail that is sloppy — a broken animation, uneven spacing, contrast that strains — erodes confidence in the AI underneath. Polish is not aesthetic indulgence; it is functional trust-building.

3. **Patterns before novelty.** Power users repeat the same workflows daily. Predictable, learnable interaction patterns are more valuable than clever surprises. Innovation earns its place by solving a real friction, not by being interesting.

4. **Dark-first, theme-aware.** The dark theme is the primary experience and sets the identity of the product. Light mode is a genuine, fully-resolved alternative, not a simple inversion.

5. **Expert-to-expert tone.** Copy is direct, specific, and assumes domain competence. No hand-holding copy, no buzzword phrases, no restated headings.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 Level AA baseline for enterprise deployment. Both dark and light themes must meet contrast requirements independently. Dark-mode contrast requires active attention given the layered near-black backgrounds. No motion-gating of content: reveal animations must enhance an already-visible default. Reduced motion alternatives required for all transitions.
